# Product

## Product Name

Go Green

## Users

Go Green is for students, families, community members, employees, and campaign organizers who want a simple and engaging way to learn about environmental responsibility. The product should also support administrators who run green campaigns, track participation, and review learning or activity outcomes.

## Product Purpose

Go Green helps people build practical environmental habits through games, missions, and learning activities about waste and sustainability. The product turns topics such as waste sorting, recycling, reuse, energy saving, and responsible consumption into approachable actions that users can complete, repeat, and share.

Success means users understand how to separate waste correctly, feel motivated to take greener actions, and can see progress from both personal and community participation.

## Core Experience

1. Learn environmental concepts through short, clear, Thai-first content.
2. Play games or complete activities related to waste sorting and sustainability.
3. Earn points, badges, or progress from green actions.
4. Join campaigns, missions, or community challenges.
5. Review participation, scores, and impact summaries.

## Key Product Areas

- Waste sorting game: users classify items into the correct waste or recycling category.
- Green missions: daily or campaign-based tasks such as reducing plastic, reusing materials, or learning a recycling rule.
- Quiz and learning cards: lightweight environmental knowledge checks.
- Rewards and progress: points, badges, levels, streaks, and completion status.
- Leaderboard or team ranking: friendly competition for schools, offices, or communities.
- Campaign dashboard: organizer view for participation, engagement, and environmental impact indicators.

## Content Themes

- General waste, recyclable waste, organic waste, hazardous waste, and electronic waste
- Reduce, reuse, recycle, and responsible disposal
- Climate and environmental impact of everyday choices
- Community cleanliness and public participation
- Local Thai context, examples, language, and campaign messaging

## Brand Personality

Friendly, optimistic, practical, and trustworthy. The interface should make environmental action feel easy to start, satisfying to repeat, and meaningful without becoming childish or preachy.

## Design Principles

1. Make the next green action obvious.
2. Use play to teach, not to distract from the environmental message.
3. Keep waste categories, rules, and feedback clear and consistent.
4. Show progress and impact in a way that feels motivating and credible.
5. Design for quick participation on mobile and comfortable review on desktop.
6. Use color with purpose: waste categories, status, progress, and rewards.
7. Keep Thai language readable, warm, and concise.

## Game Design Principles

1. Each game round should teach one clear environmental idea.
2. Feedback should explain why an answer is correct or incorrect.
3. Difficulty should increase through variety and timing, not confusing rules.
4. Scores should reward accuracy, learning, and repeated participation.
5. Failure states should encourage retrying and learning.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Do not rely on color alone to communicate waste category, score, or status. Support keyboard navigation, visible focus, sufficient contrast, reduced motion, responsive layouts, and readable Thai labels. Game interactions should be understandable without requiring fast reflexes unless a specific mode is intentionally time-based.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic corporate dashboards, overly serious environmental reports, visual clutter, excessive gamification, unclear reward mechanics, and game visuals that make the waste or environmental topic harder to understand. Do not make users guess waste categories without useful feedback.
